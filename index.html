The Silicon Brain Race

By Nadia Petrov • March 17, 2026

The chip wars are no longer a metaphor. The scramble to build the next generation of AI accelerators has become the defining technological contest of our era.

For the better part of a decade, the AI industry ran on commodity GPUs. Today, every major cloud provider maintains its own custom silicon program. Google has its TPUs, and Amazon has Trainium.

"The bottleneck is no longer the algorithm. It's the physical substrate the algorithm runs on."

Three forces converged to make custom silicon inevitable: predictable workloads, ballooning costs, and the need for lower latency. The future belongs to those who control the hardware.
